The growth of AI and its place in banks

Read time: 3 mins

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has come a long way in just a few decades, transforming industries like finance and technology. Today, AI is behind everything from fraud detection in banking to the recommendation engines on your favorite streaming platforms. The real driving force behind this evolution is machine learning (ML), with its three main approaches: supervised learning, where machines learn from labeled data, unsupervised learning, where they find patterns in raw data, and reinforcement learning, where systems learn through trial and error. Most of the AI we interact with today is what's called narrow AI, designed for specific tasks, but the idea of general AI, which can think and act like a human, remains a goal on the horizon.

'I visualise a time when we will be to robots what dogs are to humans, and I’m rooting for the machines' - Claude Shannon

As AI advances, it brings both excitement and concern about its growing role in our lives. While the benefits are clear—machines that solve problems faster and more efficiently than humans—the rapid progress raises questions. What happens when AI takes over tasks traditionally handled by people? Could we lose control over important decisions in areas like finance, healthcare, or even justice? These concerns fuel a larger debate about how much power we should give to AI and whether we can trust machines to act in our best interest.

In finance, AI is already leading the way, powering algorithmic trading, managing risk, and flagging fraud in real-time. In tech, it’s fueling advancements in areas like natural language processing (think Siri or Alexa) and computer vision (hello, self-driving cars). Yet, as we embrace these innovations, we also face important ethical questions. How much control do we hand over to machines, and how do we ensure they act in ways that align with our values? These are the kinds of discussions we need to be having as AI becomes a bigger part of our daily lives.
